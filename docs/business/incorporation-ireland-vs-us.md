# Ireland Versus United States Incorporation

Prepared: 2026-06-04

## Reader And Use

This note is for the founders and professional advisors. After reading it, the team should be able to decide what structure to discuss with a solicitor/accountant before signing customer or founder documents.

This is a business planning note, not legal, tax, accounting, securities, or immigration advice.

## Current Recommendation

Use an **Irish LTD as the parent/product company**, assign the platform IP to that Irish company, and have the three founders own shares in that Irish company. Keep Alex's existing Owl Consultancy business separate from product ownership. Add a United States subsidiary later only if there is a specific customer, hiring, procurement, or investor reason.

In short:

- Irish parent now.
- Irish company owns the platform/IP.
- Alex can still be an equal shareholder if that is the agreed founder deal.
- Owl Consultancy should be a customer, reseller, referral partner, or market partner only by separate written agreement.
- United States entity later when the need is concrete.

## Situation Assumptions

- The platform was built mainly by the Irish product team.
- The founders want the IP to remain controlled from Ireland.
- Alex is a one-third partner/founder in the commercial venture.
- Alex's value is domain insight, product feedback, validation, credibility, and US market access.
- The first external licences are expected through Alex's US investigator contacts.
- The company may apply for Irish supports while selling into the United States.

## Why Ireland Fits This Situation

### Enterprise Ireland Alignment

Enterprise Ireland's Pre-Seed Start Fund is a strong potential match for an export-focused AI software company with an MVP/beta product and early customer validation. The PSSF page says it provides EUR 50k or EUR 100k investment in the form of a convertible loan note, mentoring, and market research access. It also says applicant companies must be registered and located in Ireland, with the majority of the management team residing in Ireland and key business functions managed from Ireland.

The same Enterprise Ireland eligibility material says IP and assets required to execute the business plan that are owned by promoters must be assigned to the Irish investee company before investment completes.

That strongly favours an Irish parent if Irish funding/support is a serious path.

### Operational Reality

The centre of product development is Ireland. That matters because corporate structure should reflect where the business is actually managed, where IP is built, and where key decisions happen.

Revenue's company residency guidance says an Irish-incorporated company is generally Irish tax resident unless treated as resident elsewhere under a double tax treaty. It also says a foreign-incorporated company can be Irish tax resident if centrally managed and controlled in Ireland. That means a US parent managed from Ireland may create complexity rather than avoiding it.

### Tax And R&D Supports

Revenue states there are two main Corporation Tax rates: 12.5% for trading income and 25% for non-trading income. Revenue's 2026 R&D eBrief says Finance Act 2025 changes include increasing the R&D tax credit rate from 30% to 35%.

For a software product with continuing technical development, this makes Ireland worth exploring with an accountant.

### Founder Control And IP Clarity

Keeping IP in an Irish company does not mean excluding Alex. Her one-third economic ownership can sit at the Irish parent level. The important separation is:

- Shareholder rights belong to Alex personally or through an agreed holding structure.
- Product IP belongs to the Irish company.
- Owl Consultancy does not automatically own the platform.
- Owl Consultancy does not automatically have exclusive US sales rights.
- Any reseller/referral/customer role is documented separately.

## Why A US Parent Might Be Tempting

A US/Delaware parent can make sense when:

- US venture capital is the immediate plan.
- A major US customer refuses to contract with a non-US company.
- US hiring will start soon.
- US procurement/vendor onboarding is blocking sales.
- The team needs US banking, payroll, or investor-standard stock mechanics immediately.

Delaware C-Corps are familiar to many US startup lawyers, accelerators, and venture investors. Delaware requires a registered agent, a certificate of incorporation, and ongoing franchise tax/annual report obligations.

But this is a tool for a specific job. It is not automatically the right answer for an Irish-built product with Irish grant ambitions and only early US sales validation.

## US Parent Risks For This Team

### Foreign-Owned US Corporation Filings

If a US corporation is owned 25% or more by non-US persons, IRS Form 5472 rules may apply when there are reportable transactions with related parties. With two Irish founders each likely over 25%, this is a real compliance issue to plan for.

### US Corporate Tax And State Taxes

US C-Corps are subject to a 21% federal corporate tax rate. State tax and franchise obligations may also apply depending on structure and operations.

### Delaware Annual Costs

Delaware corporations have annual franchise tax and annual report obligations. The Delaware Division of Corporations states a minimum franchise tax under the authorised shares method and a different minimum under the assumed par value method. This is manageable, but it is another compliance calendar.

### Irish Tax Residency Complexity

If a US company is effectively directed from Ireland, Irish tax residency and permanent establishment questions may arise. This is exactly the kind of hybrid structure that needs good tax advice before implementation.

### Enterprise Ireland Risk

An Irish grant/support application may be harder if the parent company, IP, or control sits in the United States. Enterprise Ireland support is designed around Irish companies with key functions and management in Ireland.

## Recommended Structure

The preferred starting structure is:

1. Form or confirm one Irish LTD as the product company.
2. Assign all code, product IP, model prompts, deployment assets, documentation, trademarks/domain names, and product-related know-how into that Irish company.
3. Issue founder shares to the three founders according to the agreed split.
4. Put founder shares under vesting or reverse vesting, even if the split is equal.
5. Sign a shareholders agreement covering founder duties, leaver provisions, deadlock, sale, dilution, reserved matters, confidentiality, and dispute resolution.
6. Sign IP assignment and confidentiality agreements from all founders.
7. Keep Owl Consultancy separate from platform ownership.
8. Create a separate agreement if Owl Consultancy is to act as pilot customer, referral source, reseller, support partner, or US market partner.
9. Contract first US customers through the Irish LTD unless they require a US entity.
10. Revisit a US subsidiary after the first paid customer evidence is real.

## Alex-Specific Structuring Point

Alex can be an equal commercial founder without her existing business owning the product.

The distinction matters:

- **Alex as founder/shareholder:** owns part of the Irish parent and participates in upside.
- **Alex as domain partner:** provides product feedback and validation.
- **Alex as sales/channel partner:** introduces customers and may receive commission or reseller margin.
- **Owl Consultancy as customer:** uses the product for its own investigations.

These should not blur into one undocumented arrangement. If they blur, future conflicts become likely: who owns the customer relationship, who controls price, who can sign contracts, whether Alex has exclusive US rights, and whether Owl Consultancy can claim part of the platform.

## When To Add A US Subsidiary

Create a US subsidiary or Delaware C-Corp structure when one of these triggers happens:

- A signed customer says they cannot contract with an Irish company.
- The team hires US employees.
- A US enterprise procurement process requires US vendor registration.
- The team raises from US institutional investors who require a Delaware parent.
- US government or law-enforcement contracting becomes a serious route.
- US tax/accounting advice confirms the benefits outweigh the costs.

Until then, a US entity is probably premature.

## Questions For Solicitor And Accountant

Take these questions to advisors before signing founder or customer documents:

1. Can Alex be a one-third shareholder/founder in the Irish LTD without damaging Enterprise Ireland eligibility?
2. Should Alex hold shares personally, through a US entity, or through another structure?
3. How should founder vesting/reverse vesting be implemented in an Irish LTD?
4. What IP assignment wording is needed for code, prompts, data schemas, deployment assets, trademarks, and product feedback?
5. Should Owl Consultancy receive a referral agreement, reseller agreement, customer agreement, or all three?
6. Should the company avoid granting any exclusive US territory?
7. How should US customer payments to an Irish company be invoiced and documented?
8. Which US withholding/tax forms will US customers require?
9. What GDPR/data-processing position should the Irish company adopt for US-only customer data?
10. What structure best preserves eligibility for Enterprise Ireland, LEO, R&D tax credit, and future investment?

## Working Decision

Unless a professional advisor identifies a serious blocker, the working decision should be:

> Owl should be Irish-parented and Irish-IP-owned for V1, with Alex as an agreed founder/shareholder and US market partner. Add US legal infrastructure only when customer, hiring, or investor evidence justifies it.
