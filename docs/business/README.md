# Owl Business Readiness Pack

Prepared: 2026-06-04

## Reader And Use

This pack is for the founding team and close advisors. After reading it, the team should be able to decide what must happen before the first external licences, what company structure to pursue, and what the next 12 months should look like.

The current working assumption is that Owl is approaching a first V1 licensing window in July 2026 through Alex's investigator contacts in the United States, while the Irish founders also pursue Irish funding, product hardening, and a durable company structure.

## Operating View

The most sensible default path is:

- Keep the parent company and core IP in Ireland.
- Treat Alex as a founder/shareholder if that is the agreed commercial reality, but keep her existing Owl Consultancy business separate from product ownership.
- Use the first US licensing opportunities as paid validation, not as proof that the product is ready for unrestricted rollout.
- Apply for Irish supports in parallel, especially Enterprise Ireland and Local Enterprise Office routes.
- Delay a US entity until there is a specific need: US hiring, US procurement, US investor requirement, or customer refusal to contract with an Irish company.

## Review Questions

When reviewing this pack, decide:

- Which release-readiness items are true blockers before the first paid external instance?
- Which items can be handled manually for the first one to three customers?
- Whether all founders agree that the Irish company should own the platform IP.
- Whether Alex's role is founder, reseller, customer, domain partner, or some combination of these.
- Which Irish funding/support route should be pursued first.
- What price and pilot package should be offered to Alex's first contacts.
