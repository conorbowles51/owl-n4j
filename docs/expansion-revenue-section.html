A typical engagement begins with a single partner or practice group purchasing a base license with 2–3 seats. As outcomes improve and the platform proves its value on live cases, organic demand drives expansion across the organization—from 3 seats to 10, then 25, then firm-wide deployment. This viral, outcome-driven adoption is the same pattern that has driven land-and-expand leaders like Datadog and Snowflake to net revenue retention above 130%.

Why This Matters to Investors

  • Compounding, not linear growth. Each new customer becomes a growing revenue stream, not a fixed line item. Seat expansion within existing accounts compounds ARR year over year with minimal incremental cost.
  • Near-zero expansion CAC. Once a firm adopts the platform, additional seats sell themselves through internal demand. No outbound sales effort required for expansion revenue.
  • Deep switching costs. When 15+ attorneys across a firm depend on The Owl's Eye for daily casework, switching costs become prohibitive. This creates durable, defensible revenue.
  • Clear path to Series A threshold. The per-seat model creates a credible path to $10M+ ARR—the benchmark most Series A investors require—within the 5-year projection window.
  • Proven playbook. Land-and-expand is the dominant SaaS growth motion. The per-attorney ROI (10–25x) gives internal champions an easy business case to drive adoption across their organization.

Note: Projections assume conservative seat growth (3→12 seats per client over 5 years). A mid-size law firm with 50+ litigators represents a potential $200K–500K/year account. Actual expansion rates will depend on deployment velocity and customer success investment.
