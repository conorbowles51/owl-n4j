# Founder Structure And Alex Options

Prepared: 2026-06-30

## Reader And Use

This note is for the founders and advisors deciding how Alex should participate in the Irish company. After reading it, the team should be able to pick a preferred Alex structure, understand the tradeoffs, and separate founder ownership from Owl Consultancy's commercial role.

## Core Recommendation

Alex should hold her one-third founder shares personally unless her United States tax advisor gives a strong reason to use Owl Consultancy or another holding structure.

Owl Consultancy should not own the product by default. It can be separately documented as:

- A pilot customer.
- A referral source.
- A non-exclusive reseller or channel partner.
- A paid services/support partner.
- A source of domain expertise and validation.

Those are commercial roles. They should not be confused with founder equity.

## Option Comparison

| Option | Fit | Advantages | Risks |
|---|---|---|---|
| Alex personally owns one third | Best default | Clean founder story, simpler cap table, easier to explain to Enterprise Ireland and investors | Alex needs US personal tax advice and may have Irish KYC/RBO identity steps |
| Owl Consultancy owns one third | Use only if US tax advice recommends | May fit Alex's existing business administration | Blurs product ownership with services/customer role, adds corporate shareholder complexity, may complicate RBO and investor diligence |
| Alex receives options or advisor equity later | Fallback only | Useful if incorporation must happen before final deal | Does not match the stated intention that she owns one third once incorporated |
| Alex is commercial partner only | Not aligned with current intent | Simple if she is not a founder | Fails to recognise agreed one-third founder ownership |

## Alex As Personal Shareholder

This is the cleanest structure:

- Conor, Neil, and Alex are all shareholders in the Irish LTD.
- The Irish LTD owns the platform and contracts with customers.
- Alex's founder contribution is documented through the shareholders agreement.
- Owl Consultancy's market/customer role is documented separately.

Alex does not need to be a director to own shares. If she is a director, she will owe director duties to the Irish company and should understand Irish filing, governance, and conflict obligations. For Enterprise Ireland positioning, keep the company clearly located, managed, and controlled in Ireland, with the majority of management and key business functions in Ireland.

## Alex Through Owl Consultancy

This may look convenient, but it is messier.

If Owl Consultancy owns one third:

- The cap table includes a US corporate shareholder.
- The Irish company may still need to identify Alex as a beneficial owner if she controls Owl Consultancy.
- Alex's founder upside is mediated through her company, not held directly.
- The same US company may become a customer, reseller, referrer, services partner, and shareholder, which creates conflict and pricing issues.
- Future investors may ask why the founder does not hold shares personally.
- Enterprise Ireland or LEO advisors may ask for more explanation of ownership, control, and management location.

This route should be used only after Alex's US accountant/attorney explains why it is better than personal ownership.

## Alex As Director

Alex can be a shareholder without being a director. Consider making her a director only if it improves governance and reflects real decision-making.

Potential reasons to appoint Alex:

- She is genuinely part of board-level company decisions.
- Her domain expertise and US market role should be represented formally.
- Customers or investors expect all founders to be on the board.

Reasons to wait:

- Directors have legal duties and filing/KYC friction.
- A US-based director may need identity verification steps.
- Enterprise Ireland eligibility is easier to explain if company management and control remain clearly Irish.
- Founder rights can be protected through the shareholders agreement without directorship.

Default: Conor and Neil as Irish resident directors at incorporation; Alex as shareholder and founder, with board observer or director appointment rights to be agreed.

## Vesting And Leaver Provisions

Equal thirds are fair only if everyone keeps contributing. Use vesting or reverse vesting.

A common startup pattern is four-year vesting with a one-year cliff, but the solicitor should adapt this for an Irish LTD and for past contributions already made. Practical compromise:

- Some portion vests immediately to recognise work already done.
- The rest vests monthly or quarterly over future service.
- Good leaver provisions treat illness, agreed departure, or death differently from bad leaver conduct.
- If a founder leaves early, unvested shares can be bought back or converted under agreed rules.

This protects Alex as much as Conor and Neil. It prevents any one person from walking away with a permanent one third while the others keep building.

## Owl Consultancy Boundary

Write down what Owl Consultancy is and is not.

Owl Consultancy may be:

- A source of domain knowledge.
- A first pilot user.
- A US referral partner.
- A services provider helping customers use the platform.

Owl Consultancy should not automatically be:

- Owner of the product IP.
- Exclusive US distributor.
- Controller of US pricing.
- Owner of customer relationships introduced through the company.
- Holder of rights to reuse the platform outside the company.
- Entitled to founder economics beyond Alex's founder shares.

If Owl Consultancy introduces customers, use a referral or reseller agreement. If it provides paid support services, use a services agreement. If it uses the product, use a customer licence. Keep those documents separate.

## Commercial Agreement Principles

If the company gives Owl Consultancy a market role, use these principles:

- Start non-exclusive.
- Define the territory and customer segment narrowly.
- Define whether compensation is referral commission, reseller margin, services fee, or no separate fee because Alex already has founder equity.
- Require customer contracts to be with the Irish LTD unless deliberately agreed otherwise.
- Require CRM or pipeline transparency.
- Put limits on discounts and promises to customers.
- Require confidentiality and data protection compliance.
- Avoid any right to copy, fork, sublicense, or independently commercialise the software.
- Add termination rights if sales targets, support standards, or conflict rules are not met.

## IP Assignment

The Irish company should own:

- Source code.
- Deployment scripts and infrastructure configuration.
- Prompts, workflows, schemas, and templates.
- Documentation and product copy.
- Brand assets, domain names, and trademarks used for the product.
- Investigation workflow designs created for the platform.
- Product feedback and improvements contributed by founders or Owl Consultancy, subject to agreed confidentiality and customer data rules.

Be especially careful with the name "Owl". Existing docs say the product was originally built for or with Owl Consultancy and is being split into its own standalone company/product. If Owl Consultancy has brand goodwill in that name, either rebrand the product company or sign a clear trademark/domain/name licence or assignment.

## Grant Implications

The PSSF FAQ reviewed for this pack says an applicant company can have shareholders outside Ireland only if the company is located, managed, and controlled in the Republic of Ireland. That means Alex's US residency should not automatically block the route, but the company should present a clean Irish management and IP story.

Grant-facing posture:

- Irish LTD is the applicant and product owner.
- Conor and Neil lead Irish product development and company management.
- Alex is a US-based founder/domain expert/commercial lead.
- Owl Consultancy is separate from product ownership.
- Customer validation from the US is evidence of export potential, not evidence that the company is US-controlled.

Ask Enterprise Ireland or a BIC advisor directly how they prefer to see Alex's role documented.

## Founder Heads Of Terms

Before incorporation, agree a short heads of terms covering:

- Founder names and addresses.
- Intended company type and jurisdiction.
- Equal one-third economic ownership.
- Whether Alex holds shares personally or through Owl Consultancy.
- Director and secretary appointments.
- Founder roles and expected contribution.
- Vesting/reverse vesting.
- IP assignment commitment.
- Confidentiality.
- Treatment of Owl Consultancy.
- No exclusive territory unless separately signed.
- Commitment to execute shareholders agreement after incorporation.

This is not a substitute for solicitor-drafted documents, but it prevents the incorporation filing from drifting away from the real founder agreement.

## Questions For Alex's US Advisor

Alex should ask a US tax/accounting advisor:

1. Should she hold shares in the Irish LTD personally or through Owl Consultancy?
2. What US tax reporting applies if she personally owns 33.333% of an Irish company?
3. Does being a director change her US reporting position?
4. Would holding shares through Owl Consultancy create better or worse tax, accounting, or liability outcomes?
5. How should any referral, reseller, services, or consulting fees paid to Owl Consultancy be invoiced and taxed?
6. Are there US state-level issues based on where Alex lives and where Owl Consultancy is registered?
7. Are there US securities or employment issues if she receives shares subject to vesting?

## Questions For Irish Advisor

Ask the Irish solicitor/accountant:

1. Can Alex hold one third personally without harming PSSF, LEO, or HPSU eligibility?
2. What is the best share count and nominal value for equal founders and later option pool/investment?
3. How should reverse vesting be implemented in an Irish LTD?
4. What VIF/IPN steps are needed for Alex as a beneficial owner or director?
5. Should Alex be a director now, a board observer, or a shareholder only?
6. How should the company document Owl Consultancy's role without compromising IP ownership or grant eligibility?
7. Does the current product name create trademark, passing-off, or brand ownership risk?
