# Irish Incorporation And Funding Pack

Prepared: 2026-06-30

## Reader And Use

This pack is for Conor, Neil, Alex, and the solicitor/accountant or public-agency advisor helping the company form. After reading it, the founders should be able to choose the default incorporation path, brief advisors, understand Alex's options as a United States-based founder, and start the first funding applications in the right order.

This is a planning pack, not legal, tax, accounting, immigration, employment, securities, or grant-eligibility advice. The founders should verify the final structure with an Irish startup solicitor, an Irish accountant, and Alex's United States tax advisor before signing documents or applying for funding.

## Working Recommendation

Form an Irish private company limited by shares, usually an LTD, as the parent and product company. Put the platform IP, brand assets, deployment assets, documentation, know-how, and product contracts into that Irish company.

The clean default founder split is:

| Founder | Default position |
|---|---|
| Conor | One third of the Irish company |
| Neil | One third of the Irish company |
| Alex | One third of the Irish company, held personally unless US tax advice says otherwise |

Keep Alex's existing Owl Consultancy business separate from product ownership. If Owl Consultancy is a pilot customer, referral source, reseller, support partner, or US market partner, document that in a separate commercial agreement. Do not let the consultancy relationship accidentally define who owns the product.

## Why This Path Fits

- It matches where the platform is mainly built and managed: Ireland.
- It is the strongest fit for Enterprise Ireland and Local Enterprise Office support.
- It lets Alex be an equal founder without making the product a United States company.
- It keeps a United States entity optional until there is a real trigger, such as US hiring, a US customer procurement blocker, or investor requirement.
- It makes IP ownership, grant eligibility, and founder duties easier to explain to advisors and funders.

## Immediate Decisions

Decide these before filing incorporation documents or signing customer contracts:

1. Company name: avoid relying on the Owl Consultancy name unless Alex's company is deliberately licensing or assigning those rights.
2. Shareholder route for Alex: personal shareholding by default; Owl Consultancy as shareholder only if advisors recommend it.
3. Director slate: at least one Irish/EEA-resident director is needed to avoid a Section 137 bond. Conor and Neil living in Ireland should make this straightforward.
4. Secretary: an LTD with one director needs a separate secretary. With multiple directors, one director can generally act as secretary, but use an advisor or company secretarial provider.
5. Share structure: choose an equal founder share issue that leaves room for future option pools and investment.
6. Vesting or reverse vesting: even with equal thirds, use founder leaver provisions.
7. IP assignment: all founder-created product IP and product-related know-how should be assigned to the Irish company.
8. Alex/Owl Consultancy boundaries: separate founder equity from referrals, resale, services, pilot use, and US market activity.
9. Funding route: sequence New Frontiers, PSSF, LEO, Innovation Voucher, and IP advice so one support does not accidentally block another.
