# Irish LTD Setup Checklist

Prepared: 2026-06-30

## Reader And Use

This checklist is for the founder doing the company formation admin and the solicitor/accountant reviewing it. After reading it, the team should know what to decide before incorporation, what to file, and what to do immediately after the company exists.

## Preferred Entity

Use an Irish private company limited by shares, usually written as an LTD. The CRO describes an LTD as a private company that can have 1 to 149 members, may have one director, does not need to hold an AGM, and uses a one-document constitution.

For this team, the LTD is the sensible default because it is simple, grant-compatible, and familiar for Irish software startups.

## Pre-Incorporation Decisions

| Decision | Recommended default | Why it matters |
|---|---|---|
| Company type | Irish LTD | Best fit for a small product company with Irish management and export plans |
| Company name | New product/company name, not automatically "Owl Consultancy" | Avoids brand/IP confusion with Alex's existing business |
| Registered office | Ireland | Needed for Irish company administration |
| Directors | Conor and/or Neil as Irish/EEA-resident directors; Alex optional | At least one EEA-resident director avoids the non-EEA director bond issue |
| Secretary | Appoint clearly at formation | Required company officer role |
| Founder split | Equal thirds | Matches stated commercial intention |
| Share count | Use a number divisible by 3, such as 300, 3,000, or 3,000,000 ordinary shares | Avoids ugly rounding and leaves room for future option pools |
| Nominal value | Ask advisor; often small, such as EUR 0.01 or EUR 1.00 | Affects subscription amounts and admin |
| Constitution | Use advisor-prepared LTD constitution, not a random template | Should align with shareholders agreement and future funding |
| Founder vesting | Reverse vesting or leaver provisions | Protects the company if a founder stops contributing |
| IP ownership | Assign all product IP into the Irish company | Needed for funders, customers, and future investment |

## Recommended Founder Share Issue

Ask the solicitor to model this, but a simple equal issue could be:

| Founder | Shares | Percentage |
|---|---:|---:|
| Conor | 100 ordinary shares | 33.333% |
| Neil | 100 ordinary shares | 33.333% |
| Alex | 100 ordinary shares | 33.333% |

Alternatively, use 1,000,000 shares each if the advisor prefers a larger startup-style share count. The important point is not the absolute number; it is that the company records, shareholder agreement, tax treatment, and future option/investment mechanics are clean.

Do not issue a vague "one third later" promise unless incorporation absolutely cannot wait. If Alex is intended to have one third, make that explicit in founder heads of terms before incorporation and implement it in the first share issue or a tightly documented post-incorporation allotment.

## CRO Formation Steps

The core CRO route is:

1. Confirm the company type and name.
2. Prepare Form A1 and the company constitution.
3. Confirm directors, secretary, registered office, subscribers, and share capital.
4. Confirm whether a Section 137 bond is needed. With Conor and/or Neil resident in Ireland, the company should normally have an EEA-resident director and should not need the bond.
5. Submit the Form A1 and constitution through CRO/CORE.
6. Keep the signed formation records and company register documents.

Use a solicitor, accountant, or company secretarial provider if possible. The filing itself is not the hard part; the hard part is making the company documents match the founder deal, IP position, and funding plan.

## Director And Secretary Notes

- An LTD may have one director, but if it has only one director it must have a separate secretary.
- With two or more directors, one of the directors can usually act as secretary.
- At least one director should be EEA-resident unless the company is prepared to deal with the non-EEA director bond or an alternative CRO route.
- Alex can be a director, but she does not need to be a director to own shares.
- If Alex becomes a director or beneficial owner and does not have an Irish PPSN, she may need an Identified Person Number via the CRO/RBO VIF process.

## Post-Incorporation Checklist

Do these immediately after incorporation:

| Task | Timing | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Internal registers | Immediately | Members, directors, secretaries, beneficial ownership, share allotments |
| RBO filing | Within 5 months of incorporation | Each one-third founder should be a beneficial owner |
| Alex VIF/IPN | As early as possible if needed | Non-Irish PPSN beneficial owners may need VIF/IPN |
| Corporation Tax registration | Early | Ask accountant to register through ROS as appropriate |
| Bank account | Early | Bank will check CRO/RBO, KYC, founders, and source of funds |
| IP assignments | Before funding/customer contracts | Founders assign product IP to the Irish company |
| Shareholders agreement | Before customer/funding commitments | Include vesting, leaver rules, transfer restrictions, deadlock, reserved matters |
| Employment/contractor terms | Before paying founders or contractors | Founder pay should be payroll or properly documented fees, not informal withdrawals |
| First annual return | 6 months after incorporation | CRO says first B1 is due 6 months after incorporation and does not need financial statements |
| Grant evidence folder | Before applications | Store pitch deck, cost plan, customer validation, cap table, IP assignments |

## Founder Documents To Prepare

The solicitor should prepare or review:

- Founder heads of terms before incorporation if any filing happens before the final shareholders agreement.
- Shareholders agreement.
- Founder IP assignment deeds.
- Confidentiality and invention assignment terms.
- Consultancy or employment contracts if founders are paid for work.
- Board minutes for incorporation, share issue, IP assignment, and grant applications.
- Commercial agreement for Owl Consultancy if it is a customer, reseller, referral partner, or service provider.

## Shareholders Agreement Topics

Do not skip the shareholders agreement just because the split is equal. Include:

- Roles and expected contribution from each founder.
- Reverse vesting or leaver provisions.
- Good leaver and bad leaver treatment.
- Transfer restrictions.
- Reserved matters requiring unanimous or supermajority approval.
- Board composition and voting.
- Deadlock process.
- Drag-along and tag-along rights.
- Future dilution and option pool principles.
- Confidentiality.
- IP ownership and further assurances.
- Conflict rules for Owl Consultancy and any other founder business.
- Non-solicit and reasonable non-compete language only if solicitor says enforceable and appropriate.

## Grant Readiness Checks

Before applying for Enterprise Ireland or LEO support, make the company easy to diligence:

- Irish company incorporated and located in Ireland.
- Majority management and key decision-making demonstrably in Ireland.
- Cap table clear.
- Alex's US shareholding explained cleanly.
- IP assigned to the Irish company.
- Owl Consultancy relationship documented separately.
- MVP/beta evidence available.
- Customer validation evidence available.
- Export-market plan available.
- Cost plan separates salaries, product development, consultancy, legal, market research, and marketing.
- No spend incurred before grant approval where the support requires pre-approval.

## Practical Cautions

- Equal thirds can still deadlock. Decide how ordinary decisions and major reserved matters work.
- If Alex holds shares through Owl Consultancy, the Irish company may still need to identify Alex as the natural beneficial owner behind the corporate shareholder.
- Do not grant Owl Consultancy exclusive US rights casually. If exclusivity is ever offered, tie it to measurable sales targets, duration, territory, support obligations, and termination rights.
- Do not reimburse founders or repay director loans informally if PSSF is likely. Enterprise Ireland investment documents may restrict repayments to shareholders/directors.
- Do not assume a grant can be used for marketing or founder pay. Each support has its own eligible-cost rules.
