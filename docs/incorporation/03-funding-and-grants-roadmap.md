# Funding And Grants Roadmap

Prepared: 2026-06-30

## Reader And Use

This roadmap is for the founders deciding which Irish supports to pursue first. After reading it, the team should know which supports can help with modest founder pay, continued development, marketing, and later hiring, and which actions to take before spending money.

## Funding Priorities

For this company, the best funding sequence is:

1. New Frontiers, if one Irish founder can commit full-time for six months and the programme timing fits.
2. Enterprise Ireland Pre-Seed Start Fund (PSSF), once the Irish LTD, IP assignment, MVP/beta evidence, and customer validation are ready.
3. Local Enterprise Office supports, especially Feasibility, Priming, Business Expansion, and Market Explorer, checked with the local county LEO before spending.
4. Enterprise Ireland Innovation Voucher and Access Advice: IP Start for targeted research/IP work.
5. Revenue tax supports, especially R&D tax credit, Section 486C start-up company relief, and SURE if the founders' personal circumstances fit.
6. Cloud/startup credits from Google, AWS, Microsoft, and AI infrastructure providers.
7. Enterprise Ireland Innovative HPSU Fund or private seed investment later, once customer traction and a funding round justify it.

## Route 1: New Frontiers

New Frontiers is likely the best "keep one founder alive while validating" route if the timing and eligibility work.

What it offers:

- Phase 2 is a full-time six-month programme.
- The support package includes a EUR 15,000 tax-free allowance for eligible participants.
- No equity is taken.
- It includes mentoring, workshops, co-working space, market research access, and investor readiness support.

Important limitations:

- Only one co-founder can participate and receive the allowance.
- If PSSF is later accepted while receiving the New Frontiers allowance, the allowance may be cancelled at that stage.
- Existing or overlapping state funding can affect eligibility.
- The founder must be able to participate full-time.

Recommendation:

Check the nearest New Frontiers provider immediately. If an intake is open, choose the founder who can commit full-time and who benefits most from structured business validation.

## Route 2: Enterprise Ireland PSSF

PSSF is the most important near-term state investment route.

What it offers:

- EUR 50,000 or EUR 100,000 investment by convertible loan note.
- The EUR 100,000 route is paid in two EUR 50,000 tranches.
- A minimum EUR 5,000 new cash co-investment is required for each EUR 50,000 tranche.
- Mentoring and market research access through Enterprise Ireland.

Fit for Owl:

- Strong fit for an Irish AI/software product with MVP/beta functionality and US export potential.
- Useful for development, product testing, salaries, travel, consultancy, and key commercial milestones.
- Particularly compelling once there is first customer validation from Alex's network or paid pilots.

Key eligibility posture:

- Applicant company should be registered, located, managed, and controlled in Ireland.
- The company should be an existing or potential Enterprise Ireland HPSU client.
- The company should have MVP/beta evidence, customer validation, a validated proposition, innovation, internationalisation potential, employment capacity, and a credible team.
- Product IP and assets needed for the business plan should be owned by or assigned to the Irish company before investment completion.

Costs to watch:

- PSSF can support salaries, travel, consultancy fees, and related business-plan milestones.
- Direct export aid costs, including sales and marketing expenditure, are not eligible.
- Legal costs are capped in the cost plan.
- Public funding such as R&D grants or LEO supports cannot be used as the required cash co-investment.

Recommendation:

Use PSSF as the anchor after incorporation, IP assignment, and first validation evidence. Do not rush it before the founder structure is clean, because PSSF due diligence will care about cap table, IP, and company control.

## Route 3: Local Enterprise Office Supports

LEO supports are useful but must be checked with the local county office. Eligibility, application timing, regional rates, and interpretation can vary.

Most relevant supports:

| Support | Useful for | Current official headline |
|---|---|---|
| Feasibility Study Grant | Market research, consultancy, prototype/technical development, own labour research | Up to EUR 15,000; 50% in Southern/Eastern region or 60% in Border/Midlands/West region |
| Priming Grant | First 18 months, salary costs, capital, consultancy, innovation, marketing, overheads | Up to 50% of investment or EUR 150,000, but grants over EUR 80,000 are exceptional |
| Business Expansion Grant | Later expansion after start-up phase, salary, training, marketing, overheads | Up to 50% and up to EUR 80,000 normally, with exceptional cases up to EUR 150,000 |
| Market Explorer Grant | First or small-scale export market research and entry strategy | Up to EUR 10,000 or 50% of eligible costs |

Important cautions:

- Speak to LEO before spending. Many grants will not support costs already incurred.
- LEO support is for microenterprises and early-stage businesses; Enterprise Ireland client status can affect route.
- Salary support may be possible, but founder/promoter salary treatment is specific. Get written guidance.
- If New Frontiers is in play, overlapping LEO funding can affect the allowance.

Recommendation:

Contact the local LEO as soon as the county location is known. Ask specifically whether the platform is an "internationally traded service" and whether founder salary, prototype work, market research, and US market exploration are eligible.

## Route 4: Innovation Voucher

Enterprise Ireland Innovation Vouchers are good for discrete research or technical validation tasks with a knowledge provider.

What it offers:

- Standard EUR 10,000 vouchers.
- Co-funded vouchers up to EUR 20,000.
- Valid for 18 months.
- Intended for SMEs seeking expert input from third-level or registered knowledge providers.

Potential Owl uses:

- Independent AI evaluation or retrieval quality assessment.
- Security/privacy architecture review with an academic or applied research centre.
- Evidence graph methodology validation.
- Investigation workflow research.
- Prototype feature research where a knowledge provider is useful.

Recommendation:

Use this for a contained technical or research question. Do not use it as a substitute for core development funding.

## Route 5: Access Advice: IP Start

This is a strong fit before or shortly after incorporation because the product has brand, software, data, prompt/workflow, and possible trade-secret issues.

Potential uses:

- IP audit.
- Trademark/name advice.
- Prior-art or patentability screen if any part may be patentable.
- Trade-secret and copyright strategy.
- Advice on IP assignment from founders and Owl Consultancy boundaries.

Recommendation:

Prioritise an IP audit early, especially because the product has been connected to Owl Consultancy and existing docs suggest a rebrand is underway.

## Route 6: Tax Supports

These are not grants, but they can materially improve runway.

### R&D Tax Credit

Revenue guidance says qualifying R&D expenditure can qualify for an R&D Corporation Tax Credit. Current guidance for accounting periods commencing on or after 1 January 2024 is 30%, with Finance Act 2025 guidance moving to 35% for later specified-return-date periods.

Potential fit:

- Core technical uncertainty in AI-assisted investigation workflows.
- Graph extraction, entity resolution, citation quality, evidence integrity, and secure deployment research.
- Salary and overhead costs for qualifying R&D, if properly documented.

Caution:

R&D tax credit claims need evidence of scientific or technological uncertainty, systematic investigation, qualifying expenditure, and correct accounting/tax treatment. Start documentation early.

### Section 486C Start-Up Company Relief

Revenue describes this as corporation tax relief for new trading companies for the first five years they trade. The relief is linked to qualifying trade profits and employer PRSI.

Potential fit:

- Useful once the company has taxable profits and payroll.
- Not an immediate cash grant for pre-revenue development.

### SURE

SURE can provide an income tax refund to an eligible person starting a business, generally tied to investing in shares in a qualifying company and having previous PAYE income.

Potential fit:

- Worth checking if Conor or Neil has relevant prior PAYE income and will invest cash for shares.

Caution:

SURE is personal-tax specific. Ask an accountant before relying on it.

## Route 7: Cloud And Startup Credits

These are not grants, but they can reduce burn.

Apply for:

- Google for Startups Cloud Program, because the deployment model appears Google Cloud-heavy.
- AWS Activate, especially if any deployment or customer requirement points there.
- Microsoft for Startups Founders Hub, if Azure/OpenAI/customer procurement could matter.
- AI provider startup credits where available.

Use credits for:

- Isolated customer pilot instances.
- Development and staging environments.
- Security testing and backup/restore drills.
- Demo environments.

Do not let credits hide real unit economics. Track cloud cost per customer instance from the first pilot.

## Route 8: Innovative HPSU Fund

Enterprise Ireland's Innovative HPSU Fund is later than PSSF. The official page describes support for internationally focused HPSUs with MVPs and up to EUR 1.2 million.

Potential fit:

- After paid pilots/customers.
- When raising private seed investment.
- When the company can credibly show export growth and Irish job creation potential.

Recommendation:

Treat this as a 2027 or post-PSSF path unless Enterprise Ireland/BIC advisors say the company is already ready.

## Route 9: Larger RD&I And DTIF

The Enterprise Ireland RD&I Fund and Disruptive Technologies Innovation Fund are generally not the first move for a three-person pre-seed startup.

Potential fit later:

- A defined R&D project with substantial technical challenge.
- Collaboration with research bodies or other companies.
- Larger budget, matched funding, and grant administration capacity.

Recommendation:

Keep these in the "later" bucket. They may become relevant if the platform develops a deeper technical research agenda with partners.

## Recommended Funding Timeline

| Stage | Timing | Actions |
|---|---|---|
| Before incorporation | Now | Contact New Frontiers provider, local LEO, and BIC; do not incur grant-funded costs yet |
| Incorporation month | 0-4 weeks | Form Irish LTD, assign IP, file RBO, prepare cap table and founder documents |
| First validation | 1-2 months | Run structured pilot/customer validation through Alex's network; collect evidence and testimonials |
| First applications | 1-3 months | Apply or prepare for New Frontiers/LEO/Innovation Voucher/IP Start depending on timing |
| PSSF window | 2-4 months | Submit when MVP/beta, customer validation, Irish control, IP ownership, and cost plan are credible |
| Post-PSSF | 4-12 months | Use funding for salaries, product milestones, security hardening, customer validation, and seed-readiness |
| Scale | 9-18 months | Consider HPSU, private seed, Business Expansion, hiring support, or US subsidiary only if evidence supports it |

## PSSF Evidence Folder

Prepare these before applying:

- CRO company number and incorporation documents.
- Cap table.
- RBO confirmation.
- Founder IP assignments.
- Shareholders agreement or signed heads of terms.
- Product deck.
- Four-minute pitch video script.
- MVP/beta screenshots and product walkthrough.
- Customer validation notes from Alex/Owl Consultancy and prospects.
- Paid pilot/licence evidence if available.
- Export market research for US investigation/legal market.
- Three-year high-level financial model.
- Use-of-funds plan split by salaries, product development, security, travel, consultancy, and legal.
- Irish jobs plan.
- Risk register: security, data protection, AI accuracy, customer procurement, and founder dependency.

## First Calls To Make

1. Local Enterprise Office: ask about Feasibility, Priming, Market Explorer, and sequencing with New Frontiers/PSSF.
2. New Frontiers provider: ask intake dates and whether one founder can apply with two co-founders and a US founder.
3. BIC or Enterprise Ireland startup contact: ask when PSSF is appropriate and how to document Alex's US shareholding.
4. Startup solicitor: ask for incorporation, shareholder agreement, reverse vesting, and IP assignment.
5. Accountant: ask about payroll, SURE, R&D tax credit recordkeeping, CT registration, VAT, and US invoicing.
6. Alex's US advisor: ask whether personal or Owl Consultancy shareholding is better for her.
