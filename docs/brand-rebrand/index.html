Move the product toward source-grounded investigation intelligence.

The strongest direction is Sourcebound: a name that says every claim, path, entity, and finding stays attached to the material it came from. It fits the product truth better than mascot or light-beam metaphors, while carrying one important clearance risk.

Recommended center

Sourcebound

Structured evidence analysis for complex cases. Graphs, timelines, maps, financial flows, chat, reports, and agent-created artifacts stay anchored to source material. If the name cannot clear, the closest backups are Casebound and Claimbound.

What the product is

A case intelligence workspace

The codebase points to a practical investigation platform: case management, evidence upload and processing, entity extraction, knowledge graph, timeline, map, tables, financial review, Cellebrite phone-report workflows, source-cited chat, agent artifacts, reports, and admin controls.

Who it must reassure

Expert investigators

The likely first buyers are private investigation firms, criminal defense teams, white-collar investigators, forensic accounting boutiques, litigation support teams, and expert analysts who need speed without losing evidentiary control.

What the brand must avoid

Vague AI magic

The brand should not feel like a generic assistant, a surveillance toy, a courtroom gimmick, or a visual pun. It should feel calm, accountable, operational, source-backed, and ready for sensitive casework.

Ranked recommendation

Top identity directions

The names below are ranked for strategic fit, not legal availability. A formal trademark and domain clearance pass is still required before adoption.

1

Sourcebound

Best expression of the product promise: claims remain bound to source material. The risk is an active adjacent product using the same name.

2

Casebound

Closest fallback if Sourcebound is taken. It keeps the "bound" logic, shifts the emphasis to case structure, and remains natural in product UI.

3

Claimbound

Sharper for source-cited AI: every claim is tied to support. It is a little narrower than Casebound, but very close to the product promise.
