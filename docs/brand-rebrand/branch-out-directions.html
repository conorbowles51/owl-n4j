Branch-out exploration

Seven wider name and logo directions.

This pass deliberately moves away from the Sourcebound, Claimbound, spine-and-citation family. The goal is not to replace that work yet; it is to widen the field so the final choice has real contrast.

How this differs

New creative territories

Coined names

Evrin, Credra, Praeva, and Vestrae move away from literal product description and toward ownable brand sound.

Editorial marks

Averra and Vestrae test a more refined, publishing-quality identity rather than operational SaaS.

Signal energy

Corro and Praeva introduce motion, convergence, and strategic speed, which the bound-family logos avoided.

New palettes

This round leaves teal/brass behind: indigo/crimson, black/lime, oxblood/sage, cobalt/coral, aubergine/rose, and midnight/lilac/copper.

Generated branch-out logos

Seven additional logo boards

These are concept boards, not final vector marks. Each was prompted to avoid the prior vertical-source-spine motif and to explore a different palette and logo structure.

Vericord

Saved path: assets/logos/vericord-01-branch-logo-concept.png

Explanation. An interlaced cord-like V with a crimson verified line creates a technical, source-connection feel.

Usage notes. Good if the brand wants to stay close to verification while leaving the bound-family names behind.

Strengths. Clear, polished, and different from the brass-spine system.

Weaknesses. Could be read as generic enterprise connectivity; name has collision risk.

Modes. Light mode is strong; app icon is already shown. Dark mode needs a brighter line treatment.

Favicon. Good after simplifying the interlaced cord.

Exact prompt 
Use case: logo-brand
Asset type: brand exploration logo board
Primary request: Create a distinctive logo concept for VERICORD, an investigation intelligence product. The name suggests verified evidence connected by a cord or line, but do not make it look like the prior Sourcebound/Claimbound spine logos.
Style/medium: premium vector-like identity design, not an explanatory diagram.
Composition/framing: logo board with one standalone symbol, wordmark VERICORD, and a small app icon variant.
Lighting/mood: calm, precise, high-trust, slightly technical.
Color palette: deep indigo, graphite black, pale ice blue, one restrained crimson thread accent. Avoid teal/brass.
Materials/textures: flat, crisp, no 3D mockups.
Text (verbatim): "VERICORD"
Constraints: make it feel like a real enterprise brand; symbol can use an abstract verified line, braided cord, waveform, seal, or linked glyph.
Avoid: owls, eyes, magnifying glasses, shields, gavels, checkmarks, document stacks, generic graph nodes, AI sparkles, stock SaaS gradients, and the vertical source-spine motif.

Suggested next iteration

Combine the best parts

The strongest next round would be three focused directions: Evrin with a more forensic tagline, Vestrae with a slightly clearer product story, and Claimbound's mark language adapted into a less literal name. Vericord is worth keeping only if a formal clearance pass looks promising.
