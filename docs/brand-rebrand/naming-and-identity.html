Naming and identity

Sourcebound is the clearest direction, with a real clearance question.

The best names emphasize source attachment, case structure, provenance, and expert judgment. The shortlist below drops earlier directions that felt too generic or off-center.

Top semantic fit: Sourcebound Closest backup: Casebound Claim-focused backup: Claimbound Legal clearance required

Name-screening note

Do not treat this as legal availability.

A light web scan on June 30, 2026 found an active sourcebound.dev product positioned around AI knowledge assistance and document/source control, which makes Sourcebound strategically strong but legally and commercially risky. Keep it in play only if counsel can clear the mark, domains, and customer confusion risk. If clearance fails, use the same naming logic rather than starting over: Casebound is the closest broad backup, Claimbound is the sharper source-backed-AI backup, and Evidencebound is the clearest but longest literal option.

Ranked shortlist

Top five

1

Sourcebound

Best expression of source-cited intelligence. Strong product promise, memorable sound, and natural fit for citation-first workflows. Risk: active adjacent usage.

2

Casebound

Closest Sourcebound-style backup: case-native, clear, and broad enough for evidence, graph, timeline, map, financial, chat, agent, and report workflows.

3

Claimbound

Best shorter backup for source-cited AI. It says the system does not just answer; it keeps every claim attached to support.

4

Evidencebound

Very clear and serious. The main drawback is length: the wordmark feels heavy and the name sounds more literal than memorable.

5

Sourcehold

Compact, source-native, and close to the same promise. It feels a bit less natural than Sourcebound but may be worth screening.

Twelve candidate names

Sourcebound Every finding is bound to the source material that supports it. Best match for citation-first AI, graph, evidence, and report workflows. Active adjacent web presence; requires serious clearance. Every finding tied back to source. Abstract source path, bound claim glyph, or bracketed provenance mark. Plain, source-aware voice; ink, off-white, teal, brass. Build the case structure. Keep the source trail.
Casebound Complex case material brought into one bounded, reviewable structure. Closest Sourcebound-style backup and broad enough for the whole product. Some risk of sounding like case management; needs source-citation messaging. Bring complex cases into structure. Custom C/B mark, case boundary, binding gesture, or contained case seal. Operational and grounded; slate, brass, off-white, measured teal. One workspace for evidence, relationships, and findings.
Claimbound Every AI claim and human finding stays attached to evidence support. Excellent for source-backed chat, agent artifacts, summaries, and reports. Narrower than Casebound; may sound more legal than investigative. Every claim needs a source. Bound claim glyph, citation seal, or abstract C/B ligature. Sharper and more assertive; ink, teal, brass, crisp white space. Answers that stay attached to evidence.
Evidencebound Findings and workflows are tied directly to evidence. Very clear and serious for investigations, reports, and legal review. Long wordmark; more literal than memorable. Bound to evidence. Evidence seal, E/B monogram, or source-integrity mark. Formal and direct; ink, brass, teal, paper. Investigation intelligence bound to evidence.
Sourcehold The product holds sources in place behind every relationship and finding. Compact backup in the same semantic family. Less idiomatic than Sourcebound; may need explanation. Hold the source behind the finding. Contained source mark, holdfast symbol, or secure bracket. Steady and controlled; dark ink with warm source accent. Keep sources close to every conclusion.
SourceTether Each answer, entity, and relationship is tethered back to evidence. Natural metaphor for provenance and citations. Can sound technical or infrastructure-like. Tether every finding to source. Tether line, anchor point, citation knot, or source path. Clear and active; teal path, ink surfaces, brass nodes. Follow the tether from claim to source.
SourceAnchor Findings are anchored in original evidence. Trustworthy and easy to understand. Anchor imagery can feel generic or maritime if handled literally. Anchor findings in source. Abstract anchor point, grounded citation mark, or stable source frame. Stable, calm, conservative; ink, blue-gray, brass. Case intelligence anchored in evidence.
Sourceframe Frames case understanding around source evidence. Good conceptual bridge between documents, graph views, and citations. May sound like dev tooling or a visual framework. Frame every claim with source. Brackets, source panes, graph-in-frame motif. Analytical and calm; ink, blue-gray, teal. The source frame for complex investigations.
Source Ledger A record of what every claim came from and how evidence connects. Strong for auditability, financial investigations, and provenance. May over-index on finance or accounting. Keep the record behind every finding. Ledger lines, citation marks, compact source stamp. Precise, archival, controlled; ink, paper, teal. The source trail your case can stand on.
Proofbound Links conclusions to proof and reviewable support. Strong for legal-adjacent buyers and expert reports. "Proof" can overpromise and imply legal conclusions. Proof, bound to source. Bound proof line, reviewed finding seal, or abstract P/B mark. Formal and disciplined; ink, brass, restrained green. Build findings that can be reviewed.
Casebind Bind evidence, entities, events, communications, and findings into a working case. Useful prior option, but less natural than Casebound. Can sound like document management or binder software. Bring complex cases into structure. Layered case mark, spine, source nodes, or structured case icon. Concise and operational; slate, brass, white, measured teal. One workspace for evidence, relationships, and findings.
Corroborate Names the central investigative act: checking claims against independent sources. Excellent product promise, but less distinct as a brand name. Common dictionary word; harder to own and longer in UI. Show what supports the finding. Multiple source paths converging on a claim node. Direct, careful, transparent; blue-gray with brass evidence accent. Ask better questions of the evidence.
