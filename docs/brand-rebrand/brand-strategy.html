Brand strategy

A calm, accountable brand for source-cited investigation work.

The product is not just an AI chat layer. It is an evidence-processing and case-analysis workspace that turns fragmented material into structured, reviewable investigative intelligence.

Product summary

What the codebase actually sells

The platform ingests messy case material, extracts text and media signals, identifies entities and relationships, writes structured information to a Neo4j graph, keeps vector search available through ChromaDB, and presents the result through analyst workflows: cases, triage, evidence explorer, graph, timeline, map, table, financial review, Cellebrite reports, chat, agent artifacts, workspace notes, reports, and admin controls.

The most ownable product truth is not "AI investigation" in the abstract. It is source-bound structure: every entity, relationship, summary, financial item, map point, graph path, and agent answer should be traceable back to source documents or extracted media.

Audience

Who should believe it

Primary
Small and mid-sized private investigation firms handling document-heavy, relationship-heavy cases.
Secondary
Criminal defense teams, white-collar investigation groups, litigation support teams, and forensic accounting boutiques.
Internal users
Analysts, investigators, paralegals, forensic reviewers, expert witnesses, case managers, and administrators.
Buyer fear
They cannot risk opaque AI claims, data leakage, weak chain-of-source, or an interface that looks too unserious for sensitive work.

Personality

Brand values What that means in product What to avoid
Traceability Source citations, evidence IDs, file provenance, visible quote snippets, and clear source panels. Answers that appear detached from the materials.
Expert control Human review, editable entities, merge flows, confidence thresholds, and visible uncertainty. Autonomous claims that imply the system decides the case.
Evidence integrity Case isolation, audit logs, document lineage, and non-training assurances. Consumer-cloud language or vague security badges.
Practical speed Batch processing, triage, graph expansion, filters, saved views, exports, and report drafts. Speed framed as replacing analysts.
Clarity under complexity Graph, timeline, map, financial, table, and chat views that make relationships inspectable. Decorative complexity that makes the interface harder to read.

Messaging pillars

Core message architecture

1. Turn evidence into structure

Ingest PDFs, office files, CSVs, HTML, images, audio, video-derived media, and phone reports, then extract people, organisations, events, transactions, communications, locations, documents, devices, and other case entities.

2. Follow relationships across views

Use graph, timeline, map, table, financial, and Cellebrite workflows to inspect how entities, places, communications, payments, and documents connect.

3. Ask AI without losing the source

Chat and agent workflows should answer with citations, build artifacts, and make the source trail available for review.

4. Keep experts in control

Analysts merge, correct, categorise, compare, filter, export, and decide. The system accelerates judgment; it does not impersonate it.

5. Fit sensitive deployments

Single-tenant or isolated deployments, audit logging, backups, source citations, and no training on customer data should be sold plainly.

One paragraph

Sourcebound is an investigation intelligence workspace for teams working through fragmented case material. It processes evidence, extracts entities and relationships, builds reviewable case structure, and lets analysts move between graph, timeline, map, table, financial, chat, agent, and report workflows while keeping findings tied back to the source files.

Long description

Sourcebound helps investigators and analysts make sense of document-heavy, relationship-heavy cases. The platform ingests evidence, extracts text and media signals, identifies entities and relationships, and organizes the result into a source-cited case workspace. Teams can inspect the knowledge graph, follow events over time, map locations, review transactions, analyze phone-report material, ask questions with source context, create artifacts with an AI agent, and draft reports. The product is designed for expert-led casework where speed matters, but every claim still needs a source trail.
